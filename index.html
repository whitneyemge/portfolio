Capstone Project

The purpose of my capstone project is to identify areas of conflict over the use of public lands in the Pacific Northwest. This conflict arises when there are competing demands over the use of natural resources, such as land used for livestock grazing, energy development, timber harvesting, and recreation. My analysis will result in a visual representation of this conflict, and the results of this project will have implications for the ways that natural resources could be managed to reduce land-use conflict.

About Me

I am a Pacific Northwest native with an interest in the intersection of ethnography, participatory GIS, and conservation. I currently work as a GIS Analyst for the Washington State Department of Archaeology and Historic Preservation, where I enjoy a variety of cartographic and data QC assignments. When I am not at work, school, or sitting in traffic between the two, I enjoy snowboarding, travelling, and making up songs about my three cats.
